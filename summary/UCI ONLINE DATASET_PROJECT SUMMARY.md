PROJECT SUMMARY

Week 1 data analysis project.

UCI ONLIINE DATASET (https://archive.ics.uci.edu/dataset/352/online+retail)

DOWNLOAD LINK: https://archive.ics.uci.edu/static/public/352/online+retail.zip

OBJECTIVE:

To understand the driving revenue of the business by analyzing the sales data

The goal is to enhance analytical thinking skills beyond mere coding proficiency.

DATA PREPARATION

The dataset required cleaning due to several issues,, including missing customer IDs, inconsistent country labels, and duplicate records. This includes the presence of returns, which are negative values.

The returns we retained and analysed separately to ensure net revenue calculations. Country names were standardized and duplicate rows systematically removed.

KEY FINDINGS:

The UK accounts for a whopping 84% of total revenue, indicating a strong geographical concentration.

Revenue is highly concentrated among a small number of customers with top customers contributing a significant portion of total sales

Some of the highest revenue contributors such as postage related entries are non product items with can almost distort product level analysis

High selling products also show the highest return volumes, suggesting the need to evaluate the return rates and not absolute returns.

INSIGHTS

The business heavily relies on the UK market, which creates a geographic risk

Revenue concentration among a small set of customers invites potential vulnerability in case they decide to churn

Product level analysis must exclude non\_product entries in order to avoid misleading conclusions, return behavior should be evaluated relative to sales volume.

RECOMMENDATIONS

Geographical diversification is vital to expand into underperforming regions to reduce reliance on the UK

Focus on retaining high value customers as they contribute a large portion of the revenue

Investigation of the return rates on the high return products to gauge quality of expectation issues

Product reporting should be refined, Reporting non -product items such as postage fees indeed skews performance analysis.
